[Pierre Bayard - _How to Talk About Books You Haven't Read_](https://books.google.com/books?id=mouUBOg6FckC)
<bad>
<nf>nf</nf>  
Maybe I should have taken the author's advice and written this without reading
the book. :P I couldn't tell how much was serious and how much was a joke. Not a
great sign. My sense of humor may not be refined enough, but of his main points,
only one made sense to me:

1. Reading/not reading a book isn't binary but a spectrum. You can hear about a book, read parts of it, read it and then forget some of it, or even forget you read it at all. (OK.)
1. We all interpret books differently, and our interpretations are more important and valuable than the books themselves. (Um...not so much.)
1. It's usually better not to read any given book than to read it, since it's mostly unnecessary and it imposes an opportunity cost. (Um...not so much.)
1. Finally, the ultimate aim of thinking and talking about books (and other works of art) is to be creative, in particular <em>about ourselves</em>. (Creative, sure, but about ourselves? Narcissism, no thanks.)<br />
&nbsp;
