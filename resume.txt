At the end of my tenure, it stored >XXXB rows, >XXXPB of data, and served >XXXk[qps](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queries_per_second). (2005 - 2010)

Led and contributed to network management projects for Google's backbone network [AS15169](https://www.peeringdb.com/view.php?asn=15169), including command and control, monitoring, automation, topology, routing, peering, [and OpenFlow](https://www.wired.com/wiredenterprise/2012/04/going-with-the-flow-google/). (2010 - 2011)

Led Google's early use of [OpenID](https://openid.net/). Evangelized to stakeholders, [built](/2007-12-02_openid_comments_in_blogger) [and coordinated](https://blogger.googleblog.com/2007/12/openid-commenting.html) [multiple](https://openid-provider.appspot.com/) [integrations](https://openid-consumer.appspot.com/), and drove internal adoption that culminated in a full Google Accounts [provider](https://developers.google.com/identity/protocols/OpenIDConnect) and consumer. (2006)

Co-founded Google's transaction processing platform and led its storage engineering team. The platform handles OLTP, rating, billing, invoicing, payments, and revenue share. At the end of my tenure, it served >XXM customers, >XB transactions per day, and >XXkqps with 99.98% uptime. (2003 - 2006)
