<div class="wp-block-jetpack-markdown"><p>Discovered a fascinating job in late 19th century London: <a href="https://99percentinvisible.org/episode/matters-of-time/">train timekeepers.</a> Stations in cities across England wanted to keep track of London time, aka <em><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Railway_time">railway time</a></em>. Railway workers would set their watches, ride the train, get off at each stop, and show their watch to the station master, who would set a clock marked &quot;London time.&quot;</p>
<p>Clocks were imperfect and inevitably skewed, so the timekeepers did this regularly to fix them.</p>
<p>This is basically <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Network_Time_Protocol">NTP</a>, the venerable protocol for synchronizing clocks between computers connected over a network. The timekeepers even correspond to the UDP packets that NTP uses to communicate the current time over the network. Love it.</p>
